During the Winter 2018 semester, I took Ryerson's relatively new Virtual Reality course. <br>
This being a project-based course, I slung together a game using Unity3D.

Unity offers a myriad of features to toy with, and though the learning curve can be steep,
I put together a project I called "Arachnophobia". <br>

The controls are straight forward: <br>

The analog stick can be pressed down to create a raycast, which will allow the user to teleport around the map.
Releasing the analog stick will actually move the player.

The back triggers on either hand can spawn an energy ball. These balls have a timer of about 5 seconds, after which they explode.
Once spawned, the user can use the back triggers to grab them before they explode, and throw them at the spiders lurking around
the game map. <br>

The user can also use the back trigger to grab objects. Energy balls won't spawn if the player's hand is touching an interactable objects.
<br>
The spiders are actually somewhat intelligent agents, in which they find their way around the map, going from waypoint to waypoint.
Should the player get too close to them however, the spiders are actually able to detect the player, and attack them. <br>


Should a spider be able to touch the player, the game ends. <br>

The goal of the game is for the player to find the magic book in the castle, and bring it back to their start point.
